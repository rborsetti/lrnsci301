## On-slide copy

# Will Students Learn?  Here's How We Measure.

**PRE/POST ASSESSMENT**

1. How do coefficients and numbers around a variable affect the value of the variable?
2. Why is division considered the "opposite" of multiplication?
3. Why is multiplication considered the "opposite" of division?
4. How does the value of a variable change depending on what side of an equation it is on?

**IN-GAME MEASURES**  `+1 BONUS`

- Attempts per system, per team
- Hint usage (gated, so usage itself is a signal)
- Time per system and total mission time
- Physical logbook artifact (paper students hand in)

## Speaker notes (~90 words / ~36 sec at 150 wpm)

Four pre/post questions diagnose the four LOs directly. They map one-for-one: coefficients and variables (LO1), division as inverse (LO2), multiplication as inverse (LO3), variable on both sides (LO4). During play the server records attempts per system, hint usage (gated so it only appears after a wrong answer, which means usage itself is data), time per system, and we keep the physical logbook. The rubric notes in-game measures are a one-point bonus. We're claiming it.
