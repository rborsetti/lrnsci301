# Bring Them Home

**An Apollo 13 algebra rescue game**
DoLE Spring 2026 Final Showcase

`https://lrnsci301.rborsetti.com`  ·  Logbook PDF at `/logbook`

---

## The product, in one breath

A classroom rescue game for early high-school algebra students. Teams crew a crippled Apollo 13 spaceship. Every system is broken; the only way to repair each one is to find a missing value (the unknown *x*). One phone per crew + one printed *Flight Engineer's Logbook* per student. Students fold and cut a paper strip by hand to physically discover one unit, then enter the answer on the console to restore the system. When all systems are repaired the ship splashes down on Earth.

---

## The standard

**Full standard (Common Core).** Solve linear equations and inequalities in one variable, including equations with coefficients represented by letters.

**Chopped (focused) standard.** Solve one-variable linear equations by isolating the variable, represented by letters, through multiplication and division.

**Learners.** Early High School algebra students (transitioning from general math to algebra).

---

## The knowledge (4 learning outcomes)

| # | Knowledge | Procedural skill |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Find the value of a base unit | Isolating a variable through inverse operations |
| 2 | Create groups to understand that multiples of a base unit can be deduced down to the original value | Inverse operation: division |
| 3 | Repetitively add units to understand that parts of a base unit can be replicated to find the original value | Inverse operation: multiplication |
| 4 | Interpret base units in different contexts and understand how they relate | Equations with a variable on both sides |

## One turn, end to end (sample turn)

1. **System breaks.** The console shows the broken system (e.g. "SYSTEM 04: Rewire the mainframe") with the numbers it needs.
2. **Copy to logbook.** Crew copies those numbers onto the matching logbook page (same system number, same labels).
3. **Find one unit.** A student folds the paper strip in half and cuts along the crease. Each piece is one wire's power: the value of *x*.
4. **Restore the system.** Crew enters *x* on the phone. The system restores. Next one.

The math never gets done on the phone. It is discovered on paper.

---

## Pre/Post assessment (diagnostic, 4 questions)

1. How do coefficients and numbers around a variable affect the value of the variable? *(LO 1)*
2. Why is division considered the "opposite" of multiplication? *(LO 2)*
3. Why is multiplication considered the "opposite" of division? *(LO 3)*
4. How does the value of a variable change depending on what side of an equation it is on? *(LO 4)*

## In-game measures (recorded automatically)

- Attempts per system, per team
- Hint usage (gated to only appear after a wrong attempt, so usage itself is a signal)
- Time per system and total mission time
- The folded/cut/shaded logbook a teacher can collect

## How to run a session (teacher cheat sheet)

1. Open `lrnsci301.rborsetti.com` on a laptop or room projector. Click **Host**.
2. Set **mode** (Competition for a live race, Story for own-pace) and **mission length** (1 to 10 systems).
3. Hand each student a printed logbook.
4. Each team joins from one phone with the mission code shown on the room display.
5. Click **Launch the mission**. A synchronized 3-2-1 countdown plays on every device.
6. Students work each system on paper, then enter the answer on the phone.
7. When the mission ends the ship splashes down and a debrief names every algebra strategy the students just used.

**Estimated runtime:** 25 to 45 minutes depending on mission length and class size.
