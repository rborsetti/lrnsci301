Mission briefing

Inspired by Apollo 13

"Houston, we've had a problem."

April 13, 1970. Two hundred thousand miles from Earth, an oxygen tank ruptures aboard Apollo 13. Power drops, coordinates scramble, oxygen tanks scatter, and the only way to repair each system is to solve for the missing value: the unknown, x.

Today, the crew is you. Every team races the same 10 systems independently. First crew to splashdown wins.

The console and your logbook work together. The console (this screen) shows each broken system. Your printed Flight Engineer's Logbook is where you actually repair it: copy the numbers across, fold and cut the strip by hand until you can see one unit, then enter your answer here to restore the system. Each console system matches the same numbered page in your logbook.

Stuck after working it on paper? Tap Need a hint? for the equation, or Still lost? for the step-by-step. Retries are unlimited.
